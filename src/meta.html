Overview

Ennely was a video editor assistant that enabled content creators to create a first cut within minutes. For content creators working with scripted videos, editing is a tedious and time-consuming process.

Ennely took care of mundane edits, freeing up creators to concentrate on refining their videos using popular video editing software like Premiere, Final Cut and Resolve once finalized the sequence selecting the best takes from each section as divided by Ennely’s AI engine.

I was the principal designer on this project and worked in an agile team of six.

Problem

As a company closely collaborating with content creators in the sound engineering industry, our team created a survey which identified three key challenges commonly encountered by many YouTubers publishing scripted videos.

1

Time-consuming file synchronization

Content creators “waste time” when it comes to managing the footage and audio files from external microphones and multiple cameras

2

Tedious removal of filler words

Silences between takes and filler words like "um," "uh," "er," and "ah" in videos, becomes a time-consuming obstacle to creating engaging content

3

Hassle of selecting the best takes

Sifting through multiple takes to find the perfect shots slows down the video creation process and becomes frustrating

"In what ways can we support users in swiftly exporting a rough cut and empower them to concentrate on crafting something truly unique?"

User journey

Designing the user journey was a meticulous process, focusing on simplicity and speed, I helped streamline the process of merging video and audio footage from multiple sources, allowing users to swiftly create a first draft.

With our AI algorithm, the video was automatically split into sections, intelligently selecting the best takes, while still granting users the freedom to manually change them afterwards, giving the optimal way for them to move onto further editing in Adobe Premiere, Final Cut and DaVinci Resolve.

Research plan & (un)moderated user tests

I created a research plan to understand user interactions with the app and assess their ability to create a rough video cut. I designed use-case scenarios to gather feedback and conducted unmoderated user tests.

Considering the time constraints of content creators, we faced challenges in scheduling tests with them. To overcome this, we conducted moderated tests internally with unbiased colleagues and used a tool called Maze to conduct unmoderated tests with content creators.

This approach allowed us to gather feedback efficiently and accommodate the limited availability of the content creators.

Iteration based on user feedback

The moderated and unmoderated user tests yielded insights that shaped the app's further development.

In the moderated tests, conducted internally, we identified a usability issue. For example, we discovered that some users struggled to locate the way to fine tune the duration of a specific take. As a result, we reconsidered that path to enhance discoverability.

During the unmoderated tests using Maze with content creators, we discovered a strong preference for an easy-to-use media file import and organization feature. This feedback prompted us to prioritize the refinement of this functionality, ensuring a seamless experience for users when importing their media assets.

These insights from both types of tests guided our iterative design process, allowing us to streamline the user experience and address specific pain points.

Un-moderated user tests provide valuable insights by allowing users to interact with a product in their own environment and on their own terms, reflecting real-world usage scenarios. This approach offers unbiased feedback and a larger sample size, providing a broader perspective on user behavior and preferences.
